Native Plants

Check it out! We've got some cool suggestions for native plants in the U.S. These suggestions are based on the east coast, if you know of any awesome west coast native plants, we'd love to hear about them.

Rue Anemone


Thalictrum thalictroides

Rue Anemone is a small woodland flower. It’s a perennial that will bloom year after year. It prefers partial to full shade, and likes a sandy rich sandy soil that drains well. It blooms in early spring and goes until summer time when it becomes dormant. It stays on the smaller side, getting around 8inches tall, although it can get to 12inches in height. The petals are white and can also be a light pink as well. This is a slow growing plant, with a mounded form. It’s great for shady border gardens that need a delicate feature. Rue anemones is food for early pollinators that don’t rely on nectar.

Resistant to: Deer, Rabbits

Witch Alder or Fothergilla


Fothergilla Gardenii

Witch Alder, also known as Fothergilla, is a beautiful slow growing shrub. The garden variety is on the shorter side, with most only growing as high as 5 feet. It has fragrant white bottlebrush flowers that bloom in early spring. Its autumn foliage are another reason it is a popular choice. Bees, and butterflies love this pollinating shrub, and birds love to use it as cover in the autumn months. It prefers sun to part-sun and likes well draining soil.

Resistant: Deer

Columbine


Aquilegia

A happy perennial that will start blooming late spring and into summer. The flowers are mainly to attract hummingbirds, its favorite pollinators. They also draw in bee, butterflies, and moths. It's a short lived perennial, living to be around three years old. However, it will happily reseed on its own. If left to its own devices, it will create a cluster in your garden. It prefers part-sun areas, it can tolerate full sun in cooler climates.

Resistant: Deer
